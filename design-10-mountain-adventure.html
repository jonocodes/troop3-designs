Scout Values

The principles that guide every scout on their journey

Scout Oath

On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight.

Scout Law

A Scout is trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind, obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent.

Scout Mission

The mission of the Boy Scouts of America is to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Law.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about joining Pack 3

Cub Scouts is open to all youth in kindergarten through 5th grade (ages 5-10). We welcome scouts of all backgrounds, abilities, and experience levels. Family involvement is encouraged and essential to the Cub Scout experience, especially for our younger members.
Scouts are organized into dens based on their grade level, with each den led by volunteer den leaders (usually parents). Dens meet 1-2 times per month to work on adventures and rank requirements. The whole pack comes together monthly for pack meetings featuring awards ceremonies, group activities, and special events.
Cub Scouts progress through ranks based on their grade: Lion (Kindergarten), Tiger (1st Grade), Wolf (2nd Grade), Bear (3rd Grade), Webelos (4th Grade), and Arrow of Light (5th Grade). Each rank has its own unique adventures and requirements designed for that age group.
Annual registration with BSA is approximately $75 per scout. Pack dues vary but typically range from $50-100 per year to cover supplies and activities. The uniform (shirt and handbook) costs around $50-75. Financial assistance is available for families who need it - no scout is ever turned away due to cost. Contact our Cubmaster for more information.
Den meetings are typically 1-2 times per month for about an hour each. Pack meetings are once monthly for about 1-1.5 hours. Special activities like campouts, the Pinewood Derby, and service projects happen throughout the year. Families can participate as much as their schedule allows - we understand everyone is busy!
