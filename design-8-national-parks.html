About Pack 3

Welcome to Albany Cub Scouts

Since 1935, Pack 3 has been helping young scouts in Albany discover their potential through outdoor adventures, community service, and character-building activities. We're more than just a scouting program - we're a family of explorers.

Our pack serves boys and girls in grades K-5, providing age-appropriate activities that build confidence, teach life skills, and create lasting memories. Join us on the trail to adventure!

The Scout Way

The timeless values that guide every scout on their journey.

Scout Oath

"On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight."

Scout Law

"A Scout is trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind, obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent."

Scout Mission

"The mission of the Boy Scouts of America is to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Law."

Who Can Participate in Cub Scouts?

Cub Scouts is open to all youth in kindergarten through fifth grade (ages 5-10). Both boys and girls are welcome to join! The program is family-centered, meaning parents and guardians are encouraged to participate alongside their scouts.

  • Lions: Kindergarten (ages 5-6)
  • Tigers: 1st Grade (ages 6-7)
  • Wolves: 2nd Grade (ages 7-8)
  • Bears: 3rd Grade (ages 8-9)
  • Webelos: 4th Grade (ages 9-10)
  • Arrow of Light: 5th Grade (ages 10-11)

How Does Cub Scouts Work?

Cub Scouts is organized into dens and packs. A den is a small group of scouts (usually 6-8) of the same grade who meet regularly with trained adult leaders. All the dens together form our pack.

  • Den Meetings: 1-2 times per month for age-specific activities
  • Pack Meetings: Monthly gatherings where all dens come together
  • Special Events: Camping trips, Pinewood Derby, service projects, and more

What Are the Ranks in Cub Scouts?

Scouts advance through ranks as they complete age-appropriate adventures and electives. Each rank has required and elective adventures that teach different skills:

  • Lion: Introduction to scouting through family activities
  • Tiger: Building curiosity with parent partners
  • Wolf: Developing independence and new interests
  • Bear: Hands-on projects and outdoor skills
  • Webelos: Preparing for Scouts BSA with advanced skills
  • Arrow of Light: The highest Cub Scout rank before bridging

How Much Does It Cost?

Annual registration with BSA is approximately $75 per scout. Pack 3 also collects annual pack dues of $60-80 to cover materials, awards, and activities. Additional costs may include:

  • Uniform: $50-100 (required shirt, optional pants/accessories)
  • Activity Fees: Varies by event (camping, day camp, etc.)
  • Pinewood Derby Kit: ~$5

Financial assistance is available - no scout is turned away due to inability to pay. Contact our Cubmaster for confidential assistance.

What Is the Time Commitment?

Cub Scouts is designed to fit into busy family schedules. The typical commitment includes:

  • Den Meetings: 1-2 hours, 1-2 times per month
  • Pack Meetings: 1 hour, once per month
  • Special Events: A few per year (camping, derby, etc.)

We understand families have different availability. Our program is flexible, and scouts can participate at whatever level works for your family. The more you put in, the more you get out!
