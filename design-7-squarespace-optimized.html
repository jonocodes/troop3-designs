Are you ready for more s'mores, skits, badges, and outdoor fun?

Albany Cub Scouts are kids in kindergarten through fifth grade who join a pack to go hiking, play games, learn skills, make friends, and much, much more.

Pack Meetings

The whole Pack meets the first Thursday of every month at the Veterans Memorial Building, usually from 6:30 to 8pm. The entire family is welcome.

Veterans Memorial Hall, 1325 Portland Avenue

Den Meetings

Each den establishes its own meeting schedule, usually 1-2 times per month. At den meetings, Scouts work on advancements and have a great time.

For Lion and Tiger Scouts, a parent is expected to join their Scout at meetings.

Pack 3 is in the Herms District of the Golden Gate Council and is chartered by the American Legion Post 292 of Albany

Our Foundation

The Values We Live By

Scouting is built on timeless principles that guide our scouts to become responsible, ethical citizens.

The Scout Oath

On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight.

The Scout Law

A Scout is trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind, obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent.

The Scout Mission

The mission of Scouting America is to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes.

The Twelve Points of the Scout Law

Trustworthy Loyal Helpful Friendly Courteous Kind Obedient Cheerful Thrifty Brave Clean Reverent

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about joining Albany Pack 3.

Anyone in kindergarten through fifth grades! We welcome girls and boys. Albany's Pack 3 has always prided itself on being an inclusive unit that represents the diversity of our community. We are proud to be designated as an Inclusive Unit by Scouts for Equality.

Cub Scouts is a family program! The basic structure is a "pack" which includes all Scouts K-5. Each grade has "dens" that meet in smaller groups to work on adventures to earn their rank. Scouts complete adventures each year to achieve their rank.

Annual registration is $300 per child ($275 returning). Fees cover activities, badges, and a Pack t-shirt. Uniforms add ~$100 (check with us for reused gear!). A limited number of scholarships are available for those in need.

Pack meets once monthly on Thursday evenings (6:30-7:30). Dens meet 1-2x monthly. Plus 4 campouts per year (fall & spring), Pinewood Derby (January), Pancake Breakfast (February), and other activities. Lion/Tiger parents need Youth Protection Training (1 hour online).

Still have questions? Contact our Cubmaster!
