Where Adventure Begins

Albany Cub Scouts Pack 3 provides a nurturing environment where young scouts develop skills, build character, and create lasting memories through outdoor adventures.

Building Tomorrow's Leaders Today

For generations, Pack 3 has been helping young people in Albany discover the joy of scouting. Our program combines outdoor adventure, community service, and personal growth in a supportive, family-centered environment.

Through age-appropriate activities and challenges, scouts develop essential life skills while having fun with friends and family. Our core values guide everything we do:

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about joining Pack 3 and getting started with Cub Scouts.

Cub Scouts is open to all youth from Kindergarten through 5th grade (ages 5-10). Our program welcomes children of all backgrounds and abilities. Parents and guardians play an active role in the program, especially for younger scouts.

Scouts are grouped into dens by grade level, ensuring age-appropriate activities and challenges for everyone.

Cub Scouts is organized into Packs (like our Pack 3) which contain smaller groups called Dens. Each den is made up of scouts in the same grade who meet regularly with their den leader.

  • Den meetings happen 1-2 times per month for age-specific activities
  • Pack meetings bring everyone together monthly for awards and community
  • Special events like campouts, service projects, and competitions happen throughout the year

Each grade level has its own rank with unique adventures and requirements:

  • Lion (Kindergarten) - Introduction to scouting with parent participation
  • Tiger (1st Grade) - Exploring the world with curiosity
  • Wolf (2nd Grade) - Learning teamwork and outdoor basics
  • Bear (3rd Grade) - Building confidence and community service
  • Webelos (4th Grade) - Preparing for Scouts BSA
  • Arrow of Light (5th Grade) - Highest Cub Scout rank

Annual dues for Pack 3 cover BSA registration, insurance, and pack activities. The current fee structure is:

  • Annual pack dues: Approximately $150-200 per scout
  • Uniform costs (shirt, neckerchief, slide): ~$40-60
  • Optional activity fees for special events (campouts, etc.)

Financial assistance is available - no scout is turned away due to inability to pay. Please contact our Cubmaster confidentially to discuss options.

Cub Scouts is designed to fit busy family schedules:

  • Den meetings: 1-2 times per month (about 1 hour each)
  • Pack meetings: Once monthly (about 1.5 hours)
  • Special events: Optional, typically 3-4 per year

Parent involvement is encouraged but flexible. Many families find that scouting becomes quality family time rather than an additional obligation.
