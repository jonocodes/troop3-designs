Welcome to Pack 3

Building Tomorrow's Leaders Around the Campfire

Since 1935, we've been helping young scouts discover their potential through adventure, service, and friendship in Albany, California.

What Makes Pack 3 Special

At Albany Cub Scouts Pack 3, we believe every child has the spark of adventure within them. Our program ignites that spark through hands-on activities, outdoor exploration, and meaningful connections with peers and mentors.

We focus on four core areas of development that prepare scouts for success in life:

Character

Building integrity and values

Citizenship

Serving community & country

Fitness

Mind & body wellness

Leadership

Developing future leaders

Our Dens

A Den for Every Scout

Cub Scouts are organized into dens by grade level, each with its own adventures and challenges.

Lion Den

Kindergarten

Our youngest adventurers explore the world through play-based learning, simple crafts, and fun activities that introduce them to the scouting family.

Tiger Den

1st Grade

Tigers begin their scouting journey with a parent partner, learning teamwork, outdoor skills, and the joy of helping others in their community.

Wolf Den

2nd Grade

Wolves build confidence through adventures that challenge them to try new things, from cooking to camping, while developing stronger friendships.

Bear Den

3rd Grade

Bears take on greater challenges and responsibilities, exploring nature, developing practical skills, and beginning to take the lead in den activities.

Webelos Den

4th Grade

Webelos scouts prepare for the transition to Scouts BSA, earning activity badges and developing the outdoor skills they'll need for their next adventure.

Arrow of Light

5th Grade

The highest rank in Cub Scouts! Arrow of Light scouts complete their Cub Scout journey and prepare to cross over to a Scouts BSA troop.

Scout Values

The Foundation of Scouting

These timeless principles guide every scout on their journey to becoming their best self.

Scout Oath

On my honor I will do my best
To do my duty to God and my country
and to obey the Scout Law;
To help other people at all times;
To keep myself physically strong,
mentally awake, and morally straight.

Scout Law

A Scout is Trustworthy, Loyal, Helpful, Friendly, Courteous, Kind, Obedient, Cheerful, Thrifty, Brave, Clean, and Reverent.

Scout Mission

The mission of the Boy Scouts of America is to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Law.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about joining and participating in Pack 3.

Cub Scouts is open to all youth from kindergarten through 5th grade. Our pack welcomes children of all backgrounds and abilities. The program is designed to be inclusive and provides a supportive environment where every child can grow, learn, and have fun.

  • Lions: Kindergarten (5-6 years old)
  • Tigers: 1st Grade (6-7 years old)
  • Wolves: 2nd Grade (7-8 years old)
  • Bears: 3rd Grade (8-9 years old)
  • Webelos: 4th Grade (9-10 years old)
  • Arrow of Light: 5th Grade (10-11 years old)

Cub Scouts is organized into dens (small groups of 6-8 scouts of the same grade) that meet 1-2 times per month. All dens come together for pack meetings once a month for group activities, award ceremonies, and special events.

Scouts work on adventures - themed activities that teach skills and values while having fun. Completing adventures earns badges and helps scouts advance through the ranks. Parents play an important role, especially in the younger ranks, helping their scouts complete requirements and participating in activities.

Cub Scouts has six ranks, one for each grade level:

  • Lion - For kindergarteners, focusing on fun and introduction to scouting with a parent partner
  • Tiger - For 1st graders, emphasizing teamwork and discovery with adult partners
  • Wolf - For 2nd graders, building confidence through new challenges
  • Bear - For 3rd graders, developing outdoor skills and responsibility
  • Webelos - For 4th graders, preparing for Scouts BSA
  • Arrow of Light - For 5th graders, the highest Cub Scout rank

Annual registration with BSA is approximately $75-85 per year. Pack 3 dues are an additional $100 per year, which covers supplies, awards, and activities. The uniform (shirt, neckerchief, slide) typically costs around $40-60.

Some activities like campouts or special events may have additional costs. Financial assistance is available - we never want cost to be a barrier to participation. Contact our Cubmaster to discuss options.

The typical time commitment includes:

  • Den meetings: 1-2 times per month, usually 1 hour each
  • Pack meetings: Once per month, about 1.5 hours
  • Special events: A few per year (campouts, Pinewood Derby, etc.)

You can participate as much or as little as fits your family's schedule. Many families find that scouting becomes a fun way to spend quality time together. Parent involvement is encouraged and helps make the program successful!
